    • Testing, Documentation, Ownership
      • PROBLEM: Features are informally defined. Testing and documentation are often not "owned" by the project owner.
      • Engineers should talk to their leads about their proposals before posting them to get early feedback and reduce round-trip time.
      • Once the proposal gets an informal OK, it has to be fleshed out: 1-3 pages of text + loom if helpful.
      • Proposals are converted into planned efforts during planning phase.
      • Every effort will include a walkthrough phase. Where after it is done, the person owning it will walk through sync or async what was done to the tech lead or some other senior person. The walkthrough must include how the things were tested and should include the documentation.
      • In general, we want to encourage walkthroughs (looms or sync) with tech leads. We can use our best judgment to decide what deserves a walk-through. E.g., a big fix or a small improvement don't need one.
